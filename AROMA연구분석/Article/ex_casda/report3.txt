#Comments and Suggestions for Authors
1.	General comment (enhance the quality of the language). There are several grammar mistakes, and the quality of the English is too low. A thorough review from a native language speaker is suggested to improve the quality of the language
2.	In the introduction section improve the literature review - by inserting the outcome. It is suggested to include in the introduction some research articles dealing with defect detection using aware data augmentation methods.
3.	Given that ControlNet-based generation is conditioned on handcrafted features, how sensitive is CASDA to errors in ROI characterization and feature extraction bias?
4.	What is the theoretical justification for the weighting scheme (0.5, 0.3, 0.2) used in the suitability score, and has any optimization or sensitivity analysis been performed?
5.	How does CASDA compare against state-of-the-art diffusion-based augmentation methods using quantitative benchmarks beyond mAP and Dice (e.g., FID, LPIPS)?
6.	To what extent does the synthetic-to-real domain gap affect generalization when deploying the trained model on unseen industrial datasets?
7.	Why does CASDA degrade segmentation performance (Dice score) in DeepLabV3+, and how can boundary artifacts from Poisson blending be mathematically minimized?
8.	How scalable is the proposed framework to multi-material or multi-sensor datasets where defect-background relationships are more complex?
9.	What are the computational costs and inference-time implications of CASDA compared to traditional augmentation and GAN-based methods?
10.	How does the method ensure that generated defects do not introduce label noise or unrealistic patterns that bias model learning?
11.	Can the proposed morphological indices (linearity, solidity, aspect ratio) fully capture complex defect geometries, or are there limitations in representing irregular micro-defects?
12.	What is the impact of the 42.7% synthetic data ratio on overfitting, and how was the optimal augmentation proportion determined?
13.	How reproducible is the CASDA pipeline across different datasets, given the dependency on heuristic thresholds for defect classification?
14.	Why does EB-YOLOv8 show marginal or inconsistent improvement compared to YOLO-MFD, and what does this imply about model–augmentation interaction?
15.	How does the quality verification threshold (Q ≥ 0.7) influence dataset diversity versus accuracy trade-offs?
16.	Could alternative blending techniques (e.g., GAN-based refinement or diffusion inpainting) outperform Poisson blending in preserving boundary fidelity?
17.	How does CASDA perform under extreme class imbalance scenarios where minority class samples are nearly absent?
18.	What is the statistical significance of the reported performance improvements, and were multiple runs conducted to ensure robustness?
19.	How does the framework handle overlapping or interacting defects, which are common in real industrial surfaces?
20.	Can the proposed approach be extended to 3D defect detection (e.g., volumetric data), and what challenges would arise?
21.	What are the limitations of using grayscale Severstal dataset (page 6) for validating a framework intended for broader industrial applications?
22.	22. Discussion must be strengthened by comparison with results published by others.
23.	Rewrite the abstract and conclusion by incorporating the above suggestions.

Overall. Rewrite the manuscript as per the comment.
