#Abstract

Automated Visual Inspection for defect detection faces severe data sparsity and class imbalance in industrial settings. Prior context-aware synthesis frameworks like CASDA address this by encoding defect–background relationships, yet rely on handcrafted per-dataset compatibility matrices and rules, limiting scalability. We propose AROMA, an eight-stage framework that automatically derives augmentation policies from dataset complexity statistics, eliminating per-dataset re-engineering. AROMA measures defect-morphology and background-context distributions through profiling, computes a Context Complexity Index (CCI) and Morphology Complexity Index (MCI), and uses a Meta Policy Generator to select clustering and placement policies via data-driven diagnostics—automatically re-deriving the compatibility matrix and rules CASDA built by hand. Evaluated on five heterogeneous industrial datasets (textile, metal, steel, ceramic, leather) under a training-free copy-paste compositing engine with gradient-domain blending, we find that context-aware ROI selection's downstream benefit is conditional on two jointly necessary factors: baseline detection headroom and surface heterogeneity (CCI). On high-CCI datasets retaining baseline headroom (AITeX 0.44, Severstal), context-aware placement yields consistent positive direction over random selection, though not statistically significant with n=3 paired comparison. The benefit vanishes on near-ceiling baselines (MTD ~0.91, Kolektor 0.974) and near-homogeneous surfaces (MVTec Leather), where AROMA converges with random placement by construction, confirming that gain is realized only where background diversity enables placement leverage and baseline headroom permits improvement. These findings characterize context-aware ROI selection as conditionally beneficial—realized where both factors hold jointly, absent otherwise—rather than a uniform improvement.
