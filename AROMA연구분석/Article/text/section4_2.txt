##4.2. Downstream Detection Performance (AITeX, Kolektor)

Two datasets were selected to evaluate the downstream utility of AROMA under distinct data regimes: AITeX, a single-class tiled-pattern dataset with defect instances confined to one anomaly type; and Kolektor, a small-scale two-class plastic-defect dataset. These datasets represent orthogonal challenges: AITeX tests the policy under morphological homogeneity and tiled spatial structure, whereas Kolektor examines performance in the limited-data regime (36 real training images) where synthetic augmentation is most critical. Downstream detection was assessed via supervised YOLOv8n training under three augmentation conditions (Baseline: no synthetic data; Random: random ROI placement; AROMA: compatibility-aware placement) over three random seeds (1, 2, 42). Results are reported as mAP@0.5 on the shared real test set.

As shown in Tables 8 and 9, the two datasets exhibit contrasting augmentation responses: AITeX demonstrates substantial AROMA advantage, whereas Kolektor converges across methods near ceiling performance.

On AITeX (Table 8), AROMA achieves 0.4683 mean mAP@0.5, outperforming both Baseline (0.2039, +129% relative gain) and Random (0.4187, +12% relative gain). The improvement is robust across seeds: Baseline is highly unstable (std 0.1138, CI95 straddling zero), whereas both augmented methods stabilize performance. AROMA's edge over Random is modest but consistent: AROMA's tighter confidence interval and lower variance suggest more uniform placement quality across the tiled defect space. The single-class, morphologically homogeneous nature of AITeX—combined with its tiled repetitive structure—appears amenable to context-aware placement: AROMA's ability to identify and prioritize diverse background contexts within the tile geometry yields measurable gains.

On Kolektor (Table 9), baseline performance is already near-ceiling (0.974 mAP@0.5); synthetic augmentation provides only marginal gains. Random achieves 0.9938 (+2% relative), and AROMA achieves 0.9870 (−1% relative to Random, +1% absolute vs. Baseline). At this ceiling level, both placement strategies converge: the 36-image Kolektor training set, combined with high intrinsic dataset quality and balanced defect representation, leaves little headroom for contextual placement to improve detection. The high-variance seed 42 (both Random and AROMA at 0.995 or below) reveals that small-dataset randomness can dominate augmentation effects. AROMA's performance parity with Random—despite its compatibility-aware design—indicates that in high-headroom regimes (Kolektor, MTD: existing strong generalization), the placement policy contributes less than baseline dataset characteristics.

The contrast between AITeX and Kolektor validates the conditional nature of AROMA's benefit: meaningful gains emerge when baseline headroom exists (AITeX: low baseline, high improvement potential) and the defect-background compatibility structure is informative (AITeX: diverse tile contexts; Kolektor: already well-covered by random placement in a small, balanced set). No claim of universal improvement is asserted; instead, the results demonstrate that AROMA recovers value in constrained regimes where random placement leaves coverage gaps, while remaining neutral in high-headroom or data-rich scenarios.
